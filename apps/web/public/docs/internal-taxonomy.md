# INTERNAL TAXONOMY & INSTITUTIONAL STANDARDS
**DOCUMENT REF:** EV-TAX-001
**STATUS:** ACTIVE (RESTRICTED)
**LAST UPDATED:** 2026-05-12

This document serves as the authoritative reference for all archival classifications, relationship rules, hierarchy tiers, metadata standards, naming conventions, archive statuses, preservation language, translation conventions, cross-reference logic, institutional syntax, and controlled vocabulary within the Elian Voigt archival system.

## A. ARCHIVAL CLASSIFICATIONS
1. **ARCHIVAL FRAGMENT:** A discrete piece of text recovered from a degraded source. Requires provenance metadata.
2. **FIELD NOTE:** Primary observation recorded in situ. Subject to environmental degradation.
3. **PARTIAL RECONSTRUCTION:** A document assembled from multiple incomplete sources. Must note confidence level.
4. **MUNICIPAL RECORD:** Official documentation from civic authorities. Often sterilized or redacted.
5. **EVIDENTIARY TRANSCRIPT:** Verbatim record of spoken or recorded audio. Must include timestamp and anomaly markers.
6. **LINGUISTIC HOLD:** Text quarantined due to referential instability or translation hazards.
7. **RESTORATION STATUS:** Meta-document detailing the recovery process of another file.
8. **COASTAL SURVEY:** Geographic and meteorological data linked to linguistic anomalies.
9. **SITE RECORD:** Physical description of a location where an event or recovery occurred.
10. **PRESERVATION LOG:** Custodial record of document degradation over time.
11. **RECOVERED CORRESPONDENCE:** Intercepted or salvaged communication between entities.
12. **TRANSCRIPTION ANOMALY:** A record where the act of transcription altered the meaning.
13. **WITNESS ACCOUNT:** Subjective narrative, inherently unreliable.
14. **CONTAINMENT NOTICE:** Warning attached to hazardous or cognitively dangerous texts.
15. **TRANSLATION VARIANT:** Alternative reading of a text, often conflicting with the primary translation.

## B. RELATIONSHIP RULES & HIERARCHY TIERS
- **Tier 1 (Primary):** Municipal Records, Evidentiary Transcripts, Coastal Surveys. (Objective, institutional).
- **Tier 2 (Secondary):** Field Notes, Recovered Correspondence, Witness Accounts. (Subjective, experiential).
- **Tier 3 (Tertiary):** Archival Fragments, Partial Reconstructions, Translation Variants. (Derived, unstable).
- **Rule of Precedence:** Tier 1 supersedes Tier 2 in factual disputes. Tier 3 requires Tier 1 or 2 corroboration.

## C. METADATA STANDARDS
All records must include:
- `Archive ID`: Unique identifier (see Naming Conventions).
- `Status`: Current institutional state.
- `Classification`: Primary category from Section A.
- `Custodian`: Responsible entity or department.
- `Recovery Date`: ISO 8601 format.

## D. NAMING CONVENTIONS
- **Archive IDs:** `[DEPT]-[TYPE]-[NUM]` (e.g., `DDW-FRAG-001`).
- **Field Note IDs:** `FN-[SERIES]-[NUM]` (e.g., `FN-004-17`).
- **Site Records:** `SR-[COORD]-[NUM]` (e.g., `SR-65N-01`).

## E. ARCHIVE STATUSES
- **ACTIVE:** Currently under review or processing.
- **DEGRADED:** Source material is actively deteriorating.
- **CONTESTED:** Multiple conflicting versions exist; no consensus.
- **UNVERIFIED:** Provenance cannot be established.
- **MUNICIPAL HOLD:** Seized by civic authorities; access restricted.
- **INTERNAL REVIEW:** Pending custodial approval for release.
- **PARTIAL RELEASE:** Redacted version available to lower-tier personnel.
- **POST-EIGHTH:** [DEFINITION REDACTED - REFER TO PROTOCOL 8].

## F. PRESERVATION STATES
- **STABLE:** No active degradation.
- **VOLATILE:** Text shifts or alters between viewings.
- **QUARANTINED:** Isolated to prevent semantic contamination.
- **UNRESOLVED OVERLAY:** Multiple incompatible records occupy the same physical/digital space.

## G. TRANSLATION STANDARDS
- **VERIFIED:** Consensus reached by three or more linguists.
- **NON-STABLE REFERENT:** The translated word points to an object or concept that no longer exists or fluctuates.
- **APPROXIMATION:** Best-effort translation of non-standard syntax.

## H. CROSS-REFERENCE LOGIC
- Links must specify the nature of the relationship: `CORROBORATES`, `CONTRADICTS`, `SUPERSEDES`, or `DERIVED_FROM`.

## I. INSTITUTIONAL SYNTAX
- Use passive voice for custodial actions ("The record was recovered").
- Avoid emotional descriptors in metadata.
- Use precise, clinical terminology for anomalous events ("referential instability", "semantic collapse").
